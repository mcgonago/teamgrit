Today is, Wednesday, June 25th, 2003

Sitting here typing up what to do over the next couple of months.

Somehow mustered up enought drive to kick myself in the
ass and over a two month period (April 8, 2003 - June 14, 2003) go
from not having run or biked since September, up in the 165+
lb. category, to:
         
  > 1.  Getting in a few 9+ hours of riding.
  > 2.  Attempt a CCB Wedn. group ride (got dropped at the 20 mi. mark,
  >     had to ride the last 20 mi. alone and miserable).
  > 3.  Fit in a 3+ hour single morning ride before work.
  > 4.  Maintain a 1/week 15 minutes of weight training.
  > 5.  Try to get 1/week swim session.
  > 6.  Faithfully maintain some sort of daily stretch routine - to help
  >     the back out.
  > 7.  Gradually build up the # hours/week or running (up to 30 min./week
  >     right now, WOW!).
  > 8.  Go see Dr. Dave to fix the lower back (it is almost there).
  > 9.  Drop weight from 165+ to around 154.
  > 10. Yearly group ride with the KMan.
  > 11. Show up at Hyannis I and do my best - 40th overall, 10th in age
  >     group, off of 3+ miles/week running.
         
After the Hyannis I race, went to the Brownies cabin for a vacation.
Had lots of fun, though the weather could have been better, caught a
cold, went back to work, and here it is now, Wednesday, 1.5 weeks
after the race, and just starting to get over the cold/flu/strep that
I caught...
         
Basically, took almost (2) weeks off after Hyannis I. Ate a lot,
rested a lot, hung out with family a lot, now time to get back in the
groove.  
                                                  
Next race is Massachusetts Triathlon, Sharon MA, on Sunday,
August 17th.

RACE DAY                                                     RACE DAY

That leaves a good (7) weeks to get ready for the next race!
                    
Last time, the idea was to concentrate somewhat on getting the weight
from 165+ to 154, which was accomplished through training and watching
the diet.

This time though, lets say today I am at 157+. Instead of trying to
drop (1) lb/week, to get down to a 150 lb. fighting weight, why not
concentrate on running an extra 30 minutes / week from now until
race week.

Lets assume an (8) minute/mile pace. 1 hr. gives roughly (8) miles
of running. If we can get to (3.5) hours, we would be at 30+ miles/week,
which would surely give me the running base to at least hold the lead
after the bike (bike will still max out at 9+ hours/week, maybe more).

So, time to concentrate on the RUN, which will automatically translate
to lower lbs.

Also, time to start counting calories and watching what I eat. Need
to create the "nutrition hall of fame" and start storing away foods
that I would have eaten, but instead attach a "sub 20" sticker to them
and store away!
