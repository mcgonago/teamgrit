GEARING & EFFICIENCY
====================
  o The objective on the bike is to go fast, not hard
  o You seek to go fast using as little energy as possible.
  o Gearing is a critical part of this efficiency equation.
  o Optimal gearing at 90-95
  o Optimal gear - up 1-3 rpm without undue motion in the saddle
  o If can increase by 5rpm or more, not in best gear.
  o Standing threshold is several beats higher than sitting
  o Basic Road Ride Guidelines

    1. baseline HR 20-30 below for aerobic and general training rides
    2. baseline HR 30-50 below for Z1 recovery rides

  o When drafting be in one tooth easier
  o Drop out of draft zone and reconnect while in same easier gear
  o 30"-45" race like cadence/gearing

THE ZEN OF PEDALING
===================
  o Following in the footsteps of ancestral Masters, Confucious and
    Lama Tsong Khapa (LTK), I too do a lot of my technique work with
    my eyes closed.  I do this to eliminate visual distractions that
    take my focus away from the nuances of maintaining a smooth and
    efficient cadence.  With my eyes closed it is also easier to
    record sensations that can then be referenced on demand.
  o Do technique workouts with eyes closed
  o Do not push the pedal all the way through on the downstroke.
  o I sometimes imagine a thin layer of air between my foot and the
    sole of the cycling shoe.
  o Imagine that your feet are merely attached to the pedals
  o Imagine also that the pedals are spinning at an increasing rate
    of speed and that this is also creating a large degree of
    centrifugal force.
  o You can do this for 4-5 revolutions and then switch legs, and then
    descend down to zero; watch how your cadence magically increases
    with no additional effort, but simply by a change of mental focus.
  o When I race, I have a song list that I can reference for a wide
    range of situations and I find that this helps me to maintain focus
    and intensity.
  o For your quality sessions, experiment with using as little of the
    pedal stroke or touching as lightly as possible on the dominant
    phase.  It almost feels a bit like cheating because you can find
    yourself hitting target HR zones yet without the accompanying
    perceived effort.  This is another variation of one of my now
    familiar mantras "go fast, not hard."  I find this exercise to be
    particularly useful for the 2.5' interval sessions.


Trainer gear terminology
========================
  o  AT gear  =  that gear which brings HR to L1-2 for 2.5' intervals
  o  AT + 1   =  one tooth more difficult than AT gear, ie. one tooth
                 less
  o  AT + 2   =  two teeth more difficult than AT gear, ie. two teeth
                 less
