 I start the swim, get passed by a few people, have a miserable

 the 7.00 miles.

 I finish my N times, get off the bike, and start the run. At this

 tell everyone how much of an idiot I am, we packed our bags, hung

 To someone like Christopher Ramsey, who before the race told me he was

Hyannis Sprint I

June 14, 1997 Swim .25 Mi./Cycle 10 Mi./Run 3.1 Mi. Hyannis, MA

This was my first triathlon in 10+ years. My first triathlon EVER was
way back in 1984/1985 over in Rhode Island, the Sri Chinmoy Triathlon,
at Misquamicut State Park. It was a 1.25 mile swim, 60+ bike, and 15
mi. run - back then there was no such thing as a "sprint" triathlon. I
trained/competed with my good friends Phil Surette and Tom
Lowery. Phil had some mechanical problems on the bike and never
finished. Tom and I were riding awesome, but Tom flatted on one of the
major climbs, I was so focused on surviving that I did not hear him
hollering for help, and I did not realize Tom was in trouble until I
rounded the turnaround at the top of the climb, and saw Tom running up
the hill with his bike carried over his shoulder. To make a long story
short, I got off the bike in about 30th place, and at the halfway
point of the run (out 7.5 and back 7.5) Tom and I grabbed a sub
sandwich from Tom's girlfriend at the time, Robin Lux, and sat on the
curb side mowing on the sandwich and asking ourselves what the _uck
were we thinking? I proceeded to finish in about 70th place overall.

I did the DMSE Medford Triathlon way back in 1988, the first summer
not working on the Cape, but Cathy was still there. I remember being
the only person without a wet suit in the freezing rain, flatting on
the 1st lap of the bike course, and having some Joe Shmoes fixing my
flat and forcing me to continue on. I should have walked back to my
car, packed it up, and driven to the Cape, but instead I lost about
10+ minutes, got back on the bike, and during the 15 mile run, bruised
the bottom of my foot, which took me months to fully recover from.

After the Medford Triathlon I competed in a few with Chuck
Sperazza. Chuck is an awesome triathlete - top age group finisher in
Hawaii. I remember fighting for survival on the run portion, with the
badly bruised foot, and Cathy (girlfriend at the time)
running/screaming next to me to pick it up, the finish line is just
ahead.  I was barely crawling, and had to tell her to shut up. To this
day she does not let me forget that day.

Those first few Tri's got me hooked - but my real love was cycling,
and for (8) years (1989 - 1996) I competed as a USCF (United States Cycling
Federation) category III rider. I loved it. Trained 200-sometimes-400
miles/week. Competed in stage races (Fitchburg, Killington) raced with
the Northeast Bicycle Club, was club Champion in 1994 (I think it
was 1994?), and fell deeply (and still) in love with the sport. But
cycling is a tough sport, it is not always the strongest who wins, it
is sometimes who is the luckiest, and with the threat of a crash at
every finish, after a major crash in Fitchburg, I decided to hang it
up and do triathlons. If I crash, I am the only one to blame. It is
always the fastest who wins - not the luckiest. Also, right about this
time, 1997, sprint triathlons were getting big, and without swimming,
running at lunch, and commuting to work on the bike, I could be very
competitive with only 6-7 hours of training/week. Sprint Triathlons
are awesome!

So here I was, at my first race in 10+ years, and decided to race with
the Elite/Pro division. Who can beat me swimming? No one. Who can beat
me cycling? No one. So, if I can swim and bike faster than anyone, and
I hold everyone off on the run, I should get some top (3)
placings. WRONG! That first race was a serious wake up call. I got out
of the water second, transitioned to the bike in 1st, got off the bike
in 3rd, and finished the run in a 189th time of 8+ minutes/mile, and
went from 3rd to 16/18 (in the Elite division).

It would take me 1+ years to get over my "run" phobia, start getting
my 5k road race time under 18:00, and start placing in the top 10
overall.
